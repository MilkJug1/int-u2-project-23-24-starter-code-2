Why Open Source Software Matters and Your Freedom is Important


Open Source Software, What it is and Why you should care.

Section 1

Open Source software is software where it's code is freely available and open to anyone contributing. Open source is very important to how certain apps, programs, and even games function. Unfortunately, people don't know about what open source software, and think it's bad to use since anyone can contribute.

Open source is really important, it's applications that power other applications, stuff that respects settings and privacy, and it is always something you can trust(Unless it's an open source app that's backed by a company). Most open source apps collect no data whatsoever, if any do, they will always ask instead of taking. And will respect your option and not collect any, or anonymize the data. Plus, they know what their users want, and listen to them, unlike other companies (Looking at you Adobe)

Section 2

Now, you might be asking yourself, why should I switch? My software works fine for me? I don't need to switch. Well if you have been keeping up with recent times, you probably have seen more and more companies try to find more ways to scrape more data off of you(Same with the government too!)

Section 3

One of the bigger benefits of Open source software, is that everyone is able to view, read, and contribute to the code. Regardless of what it might be, everyone can. Though, depending on the software you are going to be working on, most projects have certain guidelines of what to follow for contributing. This is a massive thing because, let's say your favorite piece of software added some spywear to it, if its open-source, you can just fork it, and take it out! Or, if a company stops supporting a piece of hardware that you need or love using, then you can still keep using it without the support of the company.

Another big point for Open source software is its security. You see, since open source software usually has its code freely available. Sometimes, this can make certain pieces of software more safer, since people can edit the code whenever needed. Plus, it will always be constanly supported, even if the orignal develop stops updating it, people can make forks and change whatever they need to. Plus, unlike proprietary software, open source software always has the user in mind. Most of the time, they won't add anything dumb, or change something for whatever, if they do have to, they will let you know beforehand. Since anyone can edit, it allows people to improve software but also increase the security of software, and the other times, you don't need to worry about a company not fixing a exploit that can lead to your computer getting hacked. As, people will have already have that fixed, and or made it known. Excluding issues like XZ and Log4j, most of the other times, open source software is more reliable than proprietary software.
